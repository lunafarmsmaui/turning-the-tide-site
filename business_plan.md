## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Turning the Tides is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit retreat program serving combat veterans living with PTSD. Our retreats — set on private farmland on the island of Maui, Hawaii — combine three evidence-backed therapeutic pillars: meaningful agricultural work, outdoor ocean and land activities, and small-group peer community.

Founded by John Collins, a U.S. Army infantryman who served two combat tours in Baghdad (2008 and 2010), Turning the Tides exists because of what helped him heal: not clinical settings, but other veterans, real work, and a place that felt like home. Our program is built to give that experience to others.

We serve combat veterans of any U.S. military branch dealing with post-traumatic stress, post-deployment readjustment, and the isolation that often follows. Retreats are always free for veterans, funded by grants, individual donations, corporate sponsorships, and paid civilian wellness retreats that subsidize the veteran program.

**Year 1 goal:** Run 2-3 pilot retreats serving 12-20 veterans, establish 501(c)(3) status, secure $200k in funding, and build operational infrastructure for scale.

**Year 3 goal:** Run 6-8 retreats per year, serve 40-60 veterans annually, build a national alumni network, and establish Turning the Tides as a leading agri-therapy retreat for combat veterans.

## 3. THE PROBLEM

### Combat PTSD by the Numbers
- **20% of post-9/11 veterans** experience PTSD at some point in their lives (VA, RAND Corp.)
- **17 veterans per day** die by suicide on average (VA National Veteran Suicide Prevention Annual Report)
- **Combat veterans are 2-4x more likely** to experience PTSD than non-combat veterans
- **VA wait times** average 30+ days for mental health appointments in many regions
- **Isolation** is one of the strongest predictors of poor outcomes — and one of the most common symptoms

### Gaps in Existing Services
Conventional treatment options often fall short for combat veterans:
- Clinical settings can feel sterile and detached
- Group therapy with non-veterans struggles to address shared experience
- Pharmaceutical-only approaches manage symptoms but rarely address underlying disconnection
- Many veterans simply do not seek help — for reasons of stigma, distrust, or scheduling

### What Veterans Tell Us Works
Repeated research and personal accounts (including the founder's own) point to:
- **Peer connection** with other combat veterans
- **Physical work in nature** — particularly farming and ocean activities
- **Structured but pressure-free environments**
- **Time away from triggering daily contexts**
- **Cultural humility and warm hospitality**

## 4. THE SOLUTION: PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Turning the Tides operates 7-14 day immersive retreats for groups of 4-8 combat veterans at a time. The program rests on three pillars:

### Pillar One — Farm & Land Work
Hands-on agricultural therapy partnering with local Maui farmers and agricultural cooperatives. Veterans participate in:
- Planting, harvesting, and tending traditional Hawaiian crops (kalo/taro, ulu/breadfruit, sugarcane)
- Care for livestock — chickens, goats, bees
- Building and maintaining farm infrastructure
- Preparing and sharing meals from food they helped grow

**Therapeutic basis:** Horticultural and agricultural therapy are evidence-backed interventions for PTSD, shown to reduce cortisol, restore daily routine, and rebuild a sense of purpose.

### Pillar Two — Outdoor & Ocean Activities
Veterans engage with Maui's natural environment through:
- Surf instruction with local instructors (Ho'okipa, Launiupoko, Lahaina)
- Snorkeling at Kihei beaches, Molokini Crater, Honolua Bay
- Outrigger canoe paddling
- Hiking — Haleakalā summit/crater, Pipiwai Trail, Iao Valley, Waihee Ridge
- Whale watching (December–April)
- Yoga, meditation, and stargazing

**Therapeutic basis:** Physical activity in natural environments — particularly water-based activities — is shown to reduce hyperarousal and quiet the autonomic nervous system.

### Pillar Three — Community & Connection
Small groups of 4-8 veterans rebuild social connection and trust:
- Shared meals every day
- Optional evening fire circles for peer-led discussion
- One-on-one mentorship with veterans further along in healing
- Integration with the local Maui community
- Post-retreat alumni network for ongoing connection

**Therapeutic basis:** Peer support has consistently been identified as one of the most predictive factors in long-term recovery from combat PTSD.

## 5. FOUNDER'S STORY

John Collins served two combat tours in Baghdad, Iraq with the U.S. Army Infantry — in 2008 and again in 2010. After returning home, he experienced post-traumatic stress in ways he was not prepared for. Like many combat veterans, the weight he carried home didn't show up on an X-ray.

What helped him turn things around wasn't a single program or clinical intervention — it was other veterans. People who had been there. Who didn't need explanations. Who simply understood. Combined with time on the land and the slower pace of life on Maui, that community became the foundation of his recovery.

Turning the Tides is built directly from that experience. The program isn't theoretical — it is a structured offering of the things that John knows worked for him, designed so other combat veterans can access them too.

## 10. RISK MANAGEMENT

### Operational Risks
- **Participant injury/medical event** → mitigated by medical clearance, liability insurance, on-island medical access
- **Mental health crisis during retreat** → crisis protocol, partnerships with local mental health providers
- **Weather/natural disaster (Maui)** → flexible programming, evacuation plan, insurance

### Financial Risks
- **Donor concentration** → diversify funding sources from day one
- **Cash flow gaps** → maintain 3-month operating reserve by end of Year 1
- **Cost overruns** → 15% contingency in all budgets

### Reputational Risks
- **Misuse of donor funds** → transparent annual reports, audited financials by Year 3
- **Participant complaint** → clear application/expectation process, post-retreat surveys
- **Founder dependence** → build out program team and board to avoid single-point-of-failure
