The Strategic Case

Why Bootstrap Year 1?

The lean budget isn't the long-term plan. Years 2 and beyond, the founder gets paid, infrastructure gets built, and the budget grows. But Year 1 is about proof — showing that the program works before asking for big money.

Three reasons this matters:

1. Easier to raise

Funders are far more willing to give $5,000 to a $53k pilot than a $99k plan with founder salary. Lean budgets are credible.

2. Stronger pitch to donors

The line "Founder serves as an unpaid volunteer in Year 1 — 100% of donations reach veterans directly" wins donations. Foundations love it. Individual donors love it. Grant officers love it.

3. Faster path to launch

$53,000 is a number you can hit through a single major grant + crowdfunding + community fundraising. $99,000 takes longer to assemble. Faster funding = faster veterans served.

When Founder Compensation Begins

Founder salary is deferred to Year 2 — or earlier if Year 1 funding exceeds $90,000. This is documented in the bylaws and shared with all funders so there are no surprises.

After Year 1

Once the pilot is complete, the standard budget kicks in. Year 2 includes founder compensation, paid facilitators, and proper marketing budget. The lean year is a one-time bootstrap — not the operating model.
