Our Mission
"Turning the Tides empowers combat veterans living with PTSD through immersive retreat experiences rooted in farm work, nature, and community."

Set on the island of Maui, we create a space where veterans can heal at their own pace — reconnecting with the land, with others who understand, and with themselves. No clinical sterility. Just real work, open skies, and a community that has your back.

What We Offer

The Program

Our retreats are built around three pillars — purposeful work, natural healing, and real human connection. No lectures, no pressure. Just a structured environment where veterans can breathe again.

Farm & Land Work

Hands-on agri-therapy on our Maui farmland. Growing food, working the land, and finding purpose in daily honest work. Evidence-backed and deeply grounding for combat PTSD.

The Location

Maui, Hawaii

Our retreat is set on private land on the island of Maui — one of the most naturally therapeutic places on earth. Warm weather year-round, ocean access, and rich farmland create the perfect environment for recovery and reflection.

Far from the noise of daily life, veterans come here to reset. The aloha spirit of the local community is woven into everything we do.

How This Started

From One Combat Veteran to the Next

John Collins
Founder, Turning the Tides
U.S. Army Infantry
Baghdad, Iraq — 2008 & 2010

Turning the Tides was founded by John Collins, a U.S. Army infantryman who served two combat tours in Baghdad, Iraq — in 2008 and again in 2010. Like many combat veterans, John came home carrying weight that didn't show up on an X-ray.

What turned things around for him wasn't a clinic or a program. It was other combat veterans — people who had been there, who didn't need an explanation. It was time on the land. The slower pace of life on Maui. A community that simply understood.

Turning the Tides is built directly from that experience — a structured offering of the things we know work, designed so other combat veterans can access them too. This isn't a top-down program. It's veterans helping veterans, on land that knows how to heal.

"If it worked for me, it can work for others. That's what this is all about." — John Collins, Founder

PILLAR ONE

Farm & Land Work

Real, meaningful farm work alongside local Maui partners — honest labor that produces something tangible.

WHAT YOU'LL DO

  • Plant, harvest, and maintain crops with local farmers
  • Traditional Hawaiian agriculture (kalo, ulu, sugarcane)
  • Build/repair garden beds, irrigation, infrastructure
  • Cook and share meals from food you grew
  • Learn sustainable farming and permaculture

WHY IT WORKS

Agri-therapy is evidence-backed for combat PTSD. Physical work in nature reduces cortisol, restores routine, and rebuilds the camaraderie veterans often miss after service.

PILLAR TWO

Outdoor & Ocean Activities

Maui's ocean and mountains are part of the program — physical activity in nature is one of the most effective tools for managing combat PTSD.

OCEAN & SURF

  • Surf Ho'okipa — north shore's legendary break
  • Beginner surf at Launiupoko / Lahaina
  • Snorkel Kihei (Kamaole I/II/III, Kalama Park)
  • Snorkel Molokini Crater & Honolua Bay
  • Outrigger canoe paddling out of Kihei
  • Whale watching (Dec–Apr) & paddleboarding

HIKES & LAND

  • Haleakalā summit at sunrise (10,000 ft)
  • Sliding Sands Trail — Haleakalā crater hike
  • Pipiwai Trail / Waimoku Falls in Hana
  • Iao Valley & Waihee Ridge Trail
  • Road to Hana — full-day journey
  • Stargazing & sunrise yoga on the lanai
PILLAR THREE

Community & Connection

Small group retreats where veterans rebuild trust, share at their own pace, and reconnect with people who understand.

HOW IT WORKS

  • Small groups of 4-8 veterans per retreat
  • Shared meals from farm-fresh ingredients
  • Evening fire circles — share when you're ready
  • Peer-led discussions, no clinical pressure
  • 1:1 mentorship with veterans further along

THE ALOHA SPIRIT & AFTER

Local Maui community members weave through every retreat. Connections continue after — through our alumni network, check-ins, and reunions. Many return as mentors.
